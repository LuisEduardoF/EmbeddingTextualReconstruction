This project investigates security vulnerabilities in Artificial Intelligence systems applied to the Brazilian Judiciary, specifically analyzing the risks of **embedding inversion** in labor conciliation prediction models based on BERT.

## ğŸ¯ Problem and Motivation

The Brazilian Judiciary has adopted Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques, using models such as BERTimbau for the analysis of labor lawsuits. Although vector representations (embeddings) are often considered "safe" because they are numerical, this work investigates whether these vectors retain sufficient semantic information to allow:

- **Reconstruction of the original text** from the embeddings
- **Inference of sensitive attributes** through inversion attacks
- **Leakage of confidential information** during the inference phase

### General Objective

To experimentally evaluate the security of embeddings generated by the fine-tuned BERTimbau model, verifying the feasibility of recovering textual information from judicial processes using only their vector representations.

Expected Products

1. **Attacker Model**: Neural decoder trained to reverse embeddings in text
2. **Audit Report**: Quantification of the success rate in recovering sensitive tokens and keywords
3. **Risk Analysis**: Assessment of the sufficiency of prior anonymization and dangerous semantic correlations
